How understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

By understanding the principles of neuroplasticity we can understand that skill or intelligence can be earned through hard work and repetition. Intelligence is not hard wired into our brains at birth, but rather that we increase intelligence by constantine stimulating the brain. When we practice something over and over again, we are using the same neural pathways each time we do an activity. By constantly stimulating those neural pathways we are strengthening our brains' familiarity with that task. “Practice makes permanent”.

How can I engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for my benefit

I will use the principles of neuroplasticity throughout this course by trying my best to practice coding every day. By doing the DevAcademy work, or coding on freeCodeCamp each day, I am stimulating the neural pathways that are associated with programming. If I continue to do this daily, my skills in programming will accelerate at a very fast rate, compared to if I were to practice programming less often. This will help me stay on top of things at DevAcademy.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Mindfulness meditation is a great way to increase your neuroplasticity. By focusing on the mediation that you are doing, you reduce stress, calm your brain, and move yourself into a focussed state. Another way to increase neuroplasticity is by practicing new things, and to do them regularly. By removing the state of familiarity of routine practices, we cause our brains to focus on the new thing that we are learning. Quality sleep is also another way to increase your neuroplasticity, by ensuring you have enough time asleep, and that it is uninterrupted, you increase your focus the following day.

Growth Mindset, what it is and why it is relevant

Growth mindset is a state of mind that moves away from the mindset of ‘I can’t do that’, and helps us understand, ‘I can’t do that YET’. A strong growth mindset is relevant as in life we are always going to encounter new things, and we are probably going to suck at them at first. But with a strong growth mindset, we will slowly get better and better each day.
